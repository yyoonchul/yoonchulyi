## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. Anthropic/OpenAI may be spending more than $1000 for every $100 you pay them

1. Wierda frames LLM coding as impressive but economically unstable.
   - The article starts from Wierda's own Claude Code experiment, which produced about 40,000 lines of code and a functional but incomplete application.
   - Wierda says the tool was genuinely useful because it enabled work that would otherwise have taken far longer or not happened.
   - The central concern is that today's subscription pricing may hide large subsidies for the most compute-intensive coding workflows.
2. The article separates cheap casual AI use from expensive fault-intolerant work.
   - Simple conversations with budget or frontier models may now be close to "too cheap to meter" for many users.
   - Coding, complex reasoning, finance, health, and other correctness-sensitive domains require repeated checking and revision.
   - Wierda argues that these tasks trigger recursive, tool-using, high-effort model behavior with large visible and hidden token use.
3. Subscription plans can mask much higher API-rate economics.
   - The article says a $100/month Claude Max plan used to its weekly limit could represent more than $1000 in API-priced tokens.
   - In Wierda's own usage, $180 of subscription payments corresponded to roughly $450 of API-priced token consumption while only about 20% of weekly limits were used.
   - Extrapolating that pattern toward full agentic use produces an estimated subsidy factor near 12, though Wierda presents it as an estimate rather than audited provider data.
4. The model-quality discussion is tied to provider cost control.
   - Wierda suspects newer Opus releases after Opus 4.6 may use less recursive brute force to reduce provider losses.
   - The article argues that this could explain why some users perceive later models as weaker for certain coding tasks.
   - The larger interpretation is that Anthropic and OpenAI may be trying to preserve useful quality while reducing cash burn from heavy agentic workloads.
5. The conclusion treats coding as a constrained domain, not proof of broad intelligence.
   - Code and math give models compilers, tests, structured checks, and search loops that can constrain errors at high compute cost.
   - Wierda compares the dynamic to earlier systems that improved by exploiting structure in a specific problem space.
   - The warning is that users may be left maintaining code generated under subsidized brute force after providers raise prices, restrict usage, or change model behavior.

2. The meeting is linked to a larger Siri and Apple Intelligence reset.
   - TLDR's summary says the meeting occurred while rivals were rapidly advancing and Apple's leaders feared deeper trouble without immediate changes.
   - Public context ties the discussion to WWDC 2026, where Apple was expected to show the results of the internal reset.
   - The likely product direction includes a rebuilt Siri, a more capable Apple Intelligence platform, and stronger agent-style functionality.
3. Leadership and product ownership appear central to the story.
   - TLDR reports that the team recommended fresh leadership and suggested giving Siri to Mike Rockwell, the Vision Pro creator.
   - Techmeme-surfaced posts say Tim Cook became unusually involved in Apple's AI product direction after the Apple Intelligence setback.
   - Other public context presents Craig Federighi and Rockwell as central figures in the Siri reboot and Apple's broader AI response.
4. Apple appears to be changing prior assumptions about AI products.
   - Surfaced Bloomberg-related posts say Apple had previously resisted chatbot-style products and AI photography features.
   - The new direction reportedly includes reversals on both areas, suggesting Apple is becoming more pragmatic about consumer AI expectations.
   - Secondary summaries also point to external model partnerships, including Google Gemini, where Apple's internal models may lag.
5. The market stakes are large because Apple controls the consumer deployment surface.
   - Apple has a massive installed base across iPhone, iPad, Mac, Watch, and other devices.
   - A substantially better Siri could bring agentic workflows to mainstream users rather than only developers and AI early adopters.
