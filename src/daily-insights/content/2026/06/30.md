## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. The best response to AI slop, infinite advice, and online noise is from Robin Williams

1. Acunzo opens with _Good Will Hunting_ as a metaphor for AI-era content.
   - The essay centers on Sean's park-bench speech to Will, where book knowledge is contrasted with direct experience.
   - Acunzo reads the scene as a distinction between expertise and wisdom, theory and experience, knowing and living.
   - Will is described as brilliant but untested, while Sean carries the weight of events Sean has actually lived through.
2. The argument is that AI can know language without living a life.
   - Acunzo writes that AI has read the internet but cannot read a room, feel, or experience.
   - The danger is not only bad AI output; it is the cultural pressure to treat lived experience as less valuable than searchable knowledge.
   - The essay pushes back on tools and advice sellers that imply creators should distrust their own judgment.
3. Robin Williams's performance becomes the proof point.
   - Acunzo argues that another actor could have received the same script but would not have performed it the same way.
   - The words matter, but the meaning comes from choices shaped by the performer's life, memory, pain, timing, and embodiment.
   - This becomes Acunzo's broader claim about art: it is not only arranging information but turning internal experience into meaning others can access.
4. The essay separates art from generic information production.
   - Scientific discovery is framed as a search for external truth that someone else might eventually reproduce.
   - Art is framed as the synthesis of truth into meaning, where no two people bring the same life to the same material.
   - Acunzo extends this beyond traditional artists to bloggers, podcasters, business writers, and anyone trying to make work that resonates.
5. The practical challenge is to use personal experience more deliberately.
   - Acunzo says much online work feels like a script the creator forgot to act.
   - The memorable phrase is that AI has large language models while people have "little life moments."
   - The essay ends by urging creators to talk about who they are, because that is what an audience cannot get from a book, post, video, or AI snippet.

### 2. Five Archetypes for Future Product Teams

1. Cherny starts from the collapse of old functional boundaries.
   - The post says engineering, product, design, data science, and related functions are melting into a new kind of role.
   - Cherny uses the Claude Code team as an example of how product work may be organized by contribution pattern rather than job title.
   - The framing is speculative, but it is grounded in observed team roles at Anthropic.
2. The first two archetypes move ideas into reality.
   - The Prototyper creates many new ideas, most of which may not ship.
   - The Builder takes a prototype or idea and turns it quickly into production-grade product or infrastructure.
   - Together, these roles cover the early path from exploration to a shippable system.
3. The middle roles improve quality and product-market fit.
   - The Sweeper cleans up UI, simplifies code and systems, removes things, and optimizes performance.
   - The Grower iterates on an already-built product to improve product-market fit.
   - These roles matter once an idea has enough shape to need simplification, learning loops, and sharper customer fit.
4. The Maintainer owns maturity and scale.
   - The Maintainer makes mature systems secure, reliable, fast, and efficient as they grow.
   - Cherny notes that many people span two roles, and some span three.
   - The archetypes are not tied to formal function: designers, engineers, PMs, and data scientists can fall into different categories.
5. Product stage determines the healthy team mix.
   - New pre-PMF products need strength in Prototyping, Building, and Sweeping.
   - Growing products with PMF need Builders, Sweepers, Growers, and some Maintainers.
   - Products with strong PMF need Sweepers, Growers, Maintainers, and some Builders, suggesting future roles may be less domain-specific.

### 4. 2. 전문성 밖으로 나아가기

### 5. Lean Software Scaling Laws

1. Gwern starts with a research question about coding LLM scaling.
   - The proposal asks whether LLM perplexity over source code can estimate how predictable a codebase becomes as context size grows.
   - The practical motivation is software safety: future coding LLMs may write most software, but insecure or low-quality generated code could worsen security.
   - Gwern suggests that languages and codebases with better predictability exponents should eventually become easier for LLMs to understand, repair, and write.
2. The essay separates language priors from long-run scaling behavior.
   - Popular languages such as Python benefit from larger training corpora, so current LLMs often perform well on them by default.
   - Gwern argues that corpus size is not destiny because skills can partially transfer across languages and stronger models may lift less common languages.
   - The key question is not only which language is easiest for a short script, but which language remains tractable as systems reach hundreds of thousands or millions of lines.
3. Lean is presented as the high-upside but data-starved candidate.
   - Lean offers formal verification, strong constraints, memory-safety benefits, and the possibility of proving major correctness properties.
   - The bottleneck is that there is not much Lean source code, so LLMs are not yet strong enough to translate large complex software into Lean autonomously.
   - This creates a chicken-and-egg problem: useful Lean training data would be easier to create if large Lean codebases already existed.
4. Predictability becomes the proposed measurement proxy.
   - A poorly designed system forces readers and models to chase global state, overrides, undocumented behavior, runtime surprises, and inconsistent patches.
   - A well-architected system should become more predictable as more of the system is seen, because interfaces, types, and invariants constrain what can happen next.
   - Gwern proposes using perplexity or bits per character as a weak but measurable proxy for that system-level predictability.
5. The measurement plan looks for crossover points and failure modes.
   - The cheap version uses frozen pretrained LLMs, source corpora by language, loss measurement across long context windows, position averaging, curve fitting, and extrapolation.
   - Useful cross-checks include injected subtle bugs, compile-and-test rollouts under artificial context limits, ablations of type signatures, and minimal-context experiments.
   - Gwern predicts weak languages may win at small scale but lose on scaling exponents, while Lean may start with worse absolute loss yet eventually justify investment if the exponent is better.

### 6. Moneyball for Physical AI

1. Garg frames robot data as a capital-allocation problem.
   - The Moneyball analogy is that baseball teams once paid for visible but weakly predictive metrics, while better teams isolated the statistic that actually mattered.
   - In physical AI, Garg argues that raw operational hours are the misleading metric because robot data has direct creation cost and does not scale like mined text.
   - The essay says frontier robotics may need enormous data volumes, so the industry must price the marginal utility of each data dollar rather than celebrate volume.
2. The essay maps the main robot-data modalities.
   - Observational data is broad and relatively cheap, such as egocentric or exocentric video, but it lacks direct action supervision.
   - Interventional data is action-dense, such as teleoperation demonstrations, but it is expensive and scales with human labor.
   - Deployment data is generated by production systems, but its distribution is dictated by commercial operations rather than the model's need for novelty.
3. Scaling-law lessons make repetition and low variance look expensive.
   - More data helps by a power law with diminishing returns, while diversity can lower the error floor and expand the manifold a model covers.
   - Repetition has limited utility and can eventually hurt capability, especially when a narrow slice of data is over-sampled.
   - Rare out-of-distribution events carry high marginal utility because real-world performance is constrained by the long failure tail.
4. Deployment telemetry can become a convergence trap.
   - Production data behaves like an oil well: early anomalies may be valuable, but routine successes quickly become low-utility near-duplicates.
   - Commercially viable early deployments often require low-variance environments, which makes their data easier to collect but less useful for general foundation-model progress.
   - Garg argues that narrow deployment niches can lock companies into linear integration work unless broader observational and interventional inputs expand the capability boundary.
5. The recommended metrics replace volume with information density.
   - Teams should track marginal integration cost per novel task, task-level saturation points, distributional drift, cluster coverage, and data novelty density.
   - Interventional staging should stop at a task's saturation threshold, while deployment streams should filter for failures and out-of-distribution edge cases.
   - Garg's conclusion is that physical AI capital efficiency comes from accurately pricing data novelty, not maximizing cumulative operational hours.

   - Sweeper는 UI를 정리하고, code와 system을 단순화하며, 불필요한 것을 제거하고, performance를 최적화한다.
   - Grower는 이미 만들어진 product를 반복 개선해 product-market fit을 높인다.
   - 이 역할들은 idea가 충분한 형태를 갖춘 뒤 단순화, 학습 loop, 더 선명한 customer fit이 필요할 때 중요하다.
4. Maintainer는 성숙도와 scale을 맡는다.
   - Maintainer는 mature system을 성장에 맞춰 secure, reliable, fast, efficient하게 만든다.
   - Cherny는 많은 사람이 두 역할을 넘나들고, 일부는 세 역할까지 걸친다고 말한다.
   - 이 archetype은 formal function에 묶이지 않는다. designer, engineer, PM, data scientist가 서로 다른 category에 속할 수 있다.
5. 제품 단계가 건강한 team mix를 결정한다.
   - 새로운 pre-PMF product에는 Prototyping, Building, Sweeping 역량이 필요하다.
   - PMF를 찾고 성장 중인 product에는 Builder, Sweeper, Grower와 일부 Maintainer가 필요하다.
   - 강한 PMF가 있는 product에는 Sweeper, Grower, Maintainer와 일부 Builder가 필요하며, 이는 미래 역할이 덜 domain-specific할 수 있음을 시사한다.

### 5. Lean Software Scaling Laws

1. Gwern은 coding LLM scaling에 관한 research question에서 출발한다.
   - 이 제안은 source code에 대한 LLM perplexity가 context size 증가에 따라 어떻게 변하는지 측정해 codebase의 predictability를 추정할 수 있는지 묻는다.
   - 실천적 동기는 software safety다. 미래 coding LLM이 대부분의 software를 쓸 수 있지만, insecure하거나 quality가 낮은 generated code는 security를 악화시킬 수 있다.
   - Gwern은 더 나은 predictability exponent를 가진 language와 codebase가 결국 LLM이 이해하고, 고치고, 작성하기 쉬워질 것이라고 본다.
2. 이 글은 language prior와 장기 scaling behavior를 분리한다.
   - Python 같은 인기 language는 더 큰 training corpus 덕분에 현재 LLM이 기본적으로 잘 다루는 경우가 많다.
   - Gwern은 corpus size가 운명이 아니라고 주장한다. skill은 language 사이에서 부분적으로 transfer될 수 있고, 더 강한 model은 덜 흔한 language도 끌어올릴 수 있기 때문이다.
   - 핵심 질문은 짧은 script에 어떤 language가 쉬운지가 아니라, system이 수십만 또는 수백만 line에 도달했을 때 어떤 language가 계속 다루기 쉬운지다.
3. Lean은 high-upside지만 data가 부족한 후보로 제시된다.
   - Lean은 formal verification, 강한 constraint, memory-safety benefit, 중요한 correctness property를 prove할 가능성을 제공한다.
   - Bottleneck은 Lean source code가 많지 않아 LLM이 아직 큰 complex software를 Lean으로 autonomously translate할 만큼 강하지 않다는 점이다.
   - 이로 인해 chicken-and-egg 문제가 생긴다. large Lean codebase가 이미 있다면 유용한 Lean training data를 만들기 쉬워지기 때문이다.
4. Predictability가 제안된 measurement proxy가 된다.
   - poorly designed system은 reader와 model이 global state, override, undocumented behavior, runtime surprise, inconsistent patch를 추적하게 만든다.
   - well-architected system은 interface, type, invariant가 다음에 일어날 일을 constrain하기 때문에 system을 더 많이 볼수록 더 predictable해져야 한다.
   - Gwern은 perplexity 또는 bits per character를 system-level predictability에 대한 약하지만 측정 가능한 proxy로 쓰자고 제안한다.
5. Measurement plan은 crossover point와 failure mode를 찾는다.
   - 저비용 version은 frozen pretrained LLM, language별 source corpus, long context window의 loss measurement, position averaging, curve fitting, extrapolation을 사용한다.
   - 유용한 cross-check에는 subtle bug injection, artificial context limit 아래 compile-and-test rollout, type signature ablation, minimal-context experiment가 포함된다.
   - Gwern은 weak language가 small scale에서는 이길 수 있지만 scaling exponent에서는 질 수 있고, Lean은 absolute loss가 나쁘게 시작해도 exponent가 더 낫다면 결국 투자를 정당화할 수 있다고 예측한다.

### 6. Moneyball for Physical AI

1. Garg는 robot data를 capital-allocation problem으로 framing한다.
   - Moneyball analogy는 baseball team이 한때 눈에 보이지만 predictive power가 약한 metric에 돈을 썼고, 더 나은 team은 실제로 중요한 statistic을 분리했다는 것이다.
   - Physical AI에서 Garg는 raw operational hours가 misleading metric이라고 주장한다. robot data는 직접 creation cost가 있고 mined text처럼 scale되지 않기 때문이다.
   - 이 글은 frontier robotics가 enormous data volume을 필요로 할 수 있으므로, industry가 volume을 자랑하기보다 data dollar의 marginal utility를 pricing해야 한다고 말한다.
2. 이 글은 주요 robot-data modality를 mapping한다.
   - Observational data는 egocentric 또는 exocentric video처럼 broad하고 비교적 cheap하지만 direct action supervision이 없다.
   - Interventional data는 teleoperation demonstration처럼 action-dense하지만 expensive하고 human labor와 함께 scale된다.
   - Deployment data는 production system에서 생성되지만, distribution은 model의 novelty 필요가 아니라 commercial operation에 의해 결정된다.
3. Scaling-law lesson은 repetition과 low variance를 expensive하게 보이게 만든다.
   - More data는 diminishing return을 가진 power law로 도움을 주며, diversity는 error floor를 낮추고 model이 cover하는 manifold를 넓힐 수 있다.
   - Repetition은 utility가 제한적이고, narrow slice의 data가 over-sampled될 때 capability를 해칠 수 있다.
   - Rare out-of-distribution event는 real-world performance가 long failure tail에 의해 제한되기 때문에 높은 marginal utility를 가진다.
4. Deployment telemetry는 convergence trap이 될 수 있다.
   - Production data는 oil well처럼 작동한다. 초기 anomaly는 valuable할 수 있지만 routine success는 빠르게 low-utility near-duplicate가 된다.
   - Commercially viable early deployment는 대개 low-variance environment를 필요로 하며, 이는 data collection을 쉽게 만들지만 general foundation-model progress에는 덜 유용하다.
   - Garg는 broader observational input과 interventional input이 capability boundary를 넓히지 않으면 narrow deployment niche가 company를 linear integration work에 가둘 수 있다고 주장한다.
5. Recommended metrics는 volume을 information density로 대체한다.
   - Team은 novel task당 marginal integration cost, task-level saturation point, distributional drift, cluster coverage, data novelty density를 tracking해야 한다.
   - Interventional staging은 task의 saturation threshold에서 멈춰야 하며, deployment stream은 failure와 out-of-distribution edge case를 filtering해야 한다.
   - Garg의 결론은 physical AI의 capital efficiency가 cumulative operational hours maximization이 아니라 data novelty를 정확히 pricing하는 데서 나온다는 것이다.
