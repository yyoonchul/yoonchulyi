## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. Anthropic files to go public

1. Anthropic has entered the confidential IPO preparation phase.
   - TechCrunch reports that Anthropic, the AI lab behind Claude, submitted a confidential draft registration statement to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission.
   - The company announced the filing in a Monday blog post, according to the article.
   - Anthropic has not disclosed the number of shares to be offered or the proposed price range.
   - The company says the proposed IPO will depend on market conditions and other factors.
2. A confidential filing gives Anthropic room to prepare before public scrutiny.
   - A confidential IPO filing lets a company begin the offering process without immediately disclosing detailed financials, risk factors, or internal business information.
   - Anthropic can privately evaluate timing, structure, and readiness before filing a public S-1.
   - If Anthropic proceeds, the later S-1 would include financials, legal matters, risk disclosures, and voting-power details.
   - The confidential phase reduces immediate public pressure while the company tests whether the market is ready for the deal.
3. The filing follows a massive late-stage financing round.
   - TechCrunch says Anthropic raised $65 billion in Series H funding less than a week before the filing.
   - That round pushed the company's valuation to $965 billion, close to $1 trillion.
   - Altimeter Capital, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, Sequoia Capital, Capital Group, Coatue, and D1 Capital Partners co-led the round.
   - The investor base included institutional and strategic investors positioning ahead of a potential IPO.
4. The article places Anthropic inside a heated IPO cycle for AI and frontier tech.
   - TechCrunch describes the IPO season as already intense, with SpaceX also preparing an offering.
   - SpaceX is reportedly targeting a $2 trillion valuation and seeking to raise more than $75 billion.
   - OpenAI is also expected to file for an IPO after raising $122 billion in March at an $852 billion post-money valuation.
   - These offerings would test public-market demand for artificial intelligence, space infrastructure, and other high-growth technology bets.
5. Anthropic's business momentum strengthens the IPO narrative.
   - The company was founded in 2021 by former OpenAI employees and was once viewed as a distant competitor to OpenAI and ChatGPT.
   - TechCrunch says Anthropic has since become an AI powerhouse with major enterprise customers.
   - The article reports that Anthropic's revenue run-rate has surpassed $47 billion, up from $9 billion at the end of 2025.
   - Future growth may depend partly on broader access to Mythos, a model Anthropic previewed in April and has kept restricted because it found thousands of high-severity bugs that need fixes before public release.
