## 📋 Quick Summary

### Don't let the LLM speak, just probe it.
**Source:** James Padolsey · **Category:** AI · **Link:** [Original](https://blog.j11y.io/2026-06-10_hidden-state-probes/?utm_source=tldrai)
- James Padolsey argues that many LLM judgment tasks do not need generation because the model has already encoded the answer in its hidden state before producing a token.
- The proposed method takes the hidden state at a final seed token, around the middle-to-upper layers, and trains a small MLP or linear probe to output a calibrated probability.
- The approach turns one frozen LLM plus one tiny head into a flexible classifier whose criterion can be written in English at inference time, with cost closer to embedding-classifier economics than LLM-judge economics.

---

## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. Don't let the LLM speak, just probe it.

1. Padolsey frames hidden-state probing as an alternative to expensive LLM judges.
   - The target tasks are structural judgments over text, such as whether a speaker is describing personal struggle, sarcasm, or current sentiment after a contrastive statement.
   - Embedding classifiers often miss those distinctions because cosine similarity mainly captures topics and surface associations.
   - Standard LLM judges can answer the question, but they are slower, costlier, and produce confidence scores that are not necessarily calibrated probabilities.
2. The central claim is that the model's forward pass already contains the decision.
   - In a prompt with content, a natural-language criterion, and a cue such as "Criteria met?", the comparison is performed before the model writes any answer.
   - Padolsey describes the result as geometry in the residual stream, with generation acting as a translation layer from internal decision to prose.
   - The suggested shortcut is to stop before generation, read the hidden state at the final prompt token, and train a small output head on that representation.
3. The recipe depends on varying the natural-language criterion during training.
   - Padolsey uses a small open model, with IBM's Granite 4.0 micro named as the team's example, and recommends optionally sharpening it with LoRA.
   - The prompt ends in a seed token such as "Assessment:", which is chosen to channel the relevant geometry rather than simply terminate the text.
   - A few thousand frontier-model-generated triples of criterion, content, and label teach the probe to answer "does this content satisfy this criterion" instead of memorizing one fixed classifier.
4. Calibration and LoRA turn the readout into something operationally useful.
   - After collecting hidden states at the seed position, the team fits an MLP and uses isotonic regression so scores behave like real probabilities.
   - The optional LoRA is trained with next-token loss to write one-sentence verdicts from known labels, not to classify directly.
   - At inference time, the verdict text is never generated; the writing task is scaffolding that makes the relevant decision geometry easier for the MLP to read.
5. The post also explains practical limits around caching and context interaction.
   - KV caching can prefill long content once and cheaply test many criteria as short continuations, yielding bit-identical scores to the equivalent content-first prompt.
   - The trade-off is that cached content is encoded before the model has seen the criterion, while criterion-first prompts let every content token attend to the question throughout the forward pass.
   - Padolsey says the cached route was statistically identical on the team's real-world evals, but more complex long-form judgments may require deeper criterion-content interaction at every layer.

## 📝 상세 정리

### 1. Don't let the LLM speak, just probe it.

1. Padolsey는 hidden-state probing을 비싼 LLM judge의 대안으로 제시한다.
   - 대상 task는 speaker가 자신의 어려움을 말하는지, sarcasm인지, 대비 표현 뒤 현재 sentiment가 무엇인지 같은 text structural judgment다.
   - Embedding classifier는 cosine similarity가 주로 topic과 surface association을 잡기 때문에 이런 구분을 자주 놓친다.
   - 일반적인 LLM judge는 질문에 답할 수 있지만 더 느리고 비싸며, 출력하는 confidence score가 반드시 calibrated probability는 아니다.
2. 핵심 주장은 model의 forward pass 안에 이미 decision이 들어 있다는 것이다.
   - content, natural-language criterion, "Criteria met?" 같은 cue가 있는 prompt에서 model은 어떤 answer를 쓰기 전에 이미 비교를 수행한다.
   - Padolsey는 그 결과가 residual stream의 geometry로 존재하며, generation은 internal decision을 prose로 번역하는 layer처럼 작동한다고 설명한다.
   - 제안된 shortcut은 generation 전에 멈추고 final prompt token의 hidden state를 읽은 뒤, 그 representation 위에 작은 output head를 학습시키는 것이다.
3. 이 recipe는 training 중 natural-language criterion을 다양하게 바꾸는 데 의존한다.
   - Padolsey는 small open model을 사용하며, team의 예시로 IBM Granite 4.0 micro를 언급하고 LoRA로 선택적 sharpening을 권한다.
   - Prompt는 "Assessment:" 같은 seed token으로 끝난다. 이 token은 단순한 종료자가 아니라 관련 geometry를 channeling하도록 고른 것이다.
   - Frontier model이 만든 criterion, content, label triple 수천 개는 probe가 하나의 fixed classifier를 외우는 대신 "이 content가 이 criterion을 만족하는가"를 읽게 만든다.
4. Calibration과 LoRA는 이 readout을 운영 가능한 도구로 만든다.
   - Seed position의 hidden state를 모은 뒤 team은 MLP를 fit하고 isotonic regression을 사용해 score가 실제 probability처럼 동작하게 한다.
   - Optional LoRA는 직접 classify하도록 학습되지 않고, known label에서 one-sentence verdict를 쓰도록 next-token loss로 학습된다.
   - Inference 시점에는 verdict text를 전혀 생성하지 않는다. writing task는 MLP가 관련 decision geometry를 더 쉽게 읽도록 만드는 scaffolding이다.
5. 글은 caching과 context interaction을 둘러싼 실무적 한계도 설명한다.
   - KV caching은 긴 content를 한 번 prefill한 뒤 여러 criterion을 짧은 continuation으로 싸게 테스트할 수 있고, 동등한 content-first prompt와 bit-identical score를 낸다.
   - Trade-off는 cached content가 criterion을 보기 전에 encoding된다는 점이다. 반면 criterion-first prompt는 모든 content token이 forward pass 전체에서 question에 attend할 수 있게 한다.
   - Padolsey는 team의 real-world eval에서는 cached route가 통계적으로 동일했다고 말하지만, 더 복잡한 long-form judgment는 모든 layer에서 더 깊은 criterion-content interaction을 필요로 할 수 있다.
