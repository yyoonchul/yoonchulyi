## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. Agentic Engineering

1. The essay starts by narrowing what “vibe coding” actually means.
   - Osmani defines vibe coding as prompting, accepting generated code, running it, and feeding errors back without reading the diffs.
   - He treats it as useful for prototypes, hackathon demos, personal scripts, learning, and creative exploration.
   - The boundary is code ownership: if the human cannot explain or maintain the result, it is not engineering.
2. The main concern is that one term now covers incompatible practices.
   - “Vibe coding” is being used for both quick experiments and disciplined professional workflows.
   - Osmani argues this confusion causes real operational damage because executives and teams misunderstand the risk profile.
   - The same AI output can be harmless in a weekend demo and dangerous in a payment system or production service.
3. “Agentic engineering” is proposed as the professional label.
   - The term describes orchestration of coding agents that can implement, test, and refine code under human direction.
   - The human still acts as architect, reviewer, and decision-maker, even if little code is typed by hand.
   - Osmani prefers it because it sounds professionally legible and draws a cleaner line than “vibe engineering.”
4. The workflow depends on ordinary engineering discipline.
   - Teams start with a plan, design doc, or spec before asking agents to implement.
   - They give agents scoped tasks and review the resulting code with pull-request-level rigor.
   - Tests are the key mechanism that turns unreliable agent iteration into a more reliable development loop.
5. The skill implications are uneven across experience levels.
   - Senior engineers benefit more because they can spot weak architecture, security issues, and brittle abstractions quickly.
   - Juniors can produce code before understanding it, which creates a risk of skill atrophy.
   - Osmani’s conclusion is that AI-assisted development makes fundamentals more important, not less important.

### 2. Google unveils Whoop-like screenless Fitbit Air

1. Google is positioning Fitbit Air as a simpler wearable category.
   - The device is screenless, which makes the comparison to Whoop natural.
   - It is priced at $100 and is aimed at people who find normal wearables bulky, complicated, or expensive.
   - Google’s pitch emphasizes comfort and continuous wear rather than smartwatch-style interaction.
2. The health-tracking feature set is broad for a small device.
   - Fitbit Air includes 24/7 heart-rate monitoring and heart-rhythm monitoring with A-fib alerts.
   - It also tracks blood oxygen, resting heart rate, heart-rate variability, sleep stages, and sleep duration.
   - Common activities and workouts are detected automatically, and Google says personalization improves as it learns user habits.
3. The physical design is the core product change.
   - TechCrunch reports that Fitbit Air is 25% smaller than Fitbit Luxe and 50% smaller than Inspire 3.
   - It weighs 12 grams with the band and 5.2 grams without the band.
   - Google says it can pair with Pixel Watch, letting users switch to Fitbit Air at night or during workouts.
4. Battery and durability support the always-on use case.
   - Fitbit Air has up to one week of battery life.
   - Five minutes of fast charging can provide a full day of power.
   - The device is water-resistant up to 50 meters, which fits workout and sleep-tracking use.
5. The launch is tied to a broader Google Health update.
   - Users track health and fitness through Google Health, the rebranded Fitbit app.
   - Google Health Coach, a Gemini-powered trainer and wellness advisor, is available for Google Health Premium subscribers.
   - Fitbit Air is available for preorder now and goes on sale May 26 with Performance Loop, Active, and Elevated Modern band options.

### 3. Higher usage limits for Claude and a compute deal with SpaceX

1. Anthropic links higher Claude limits directly to new compute capacity.
   - The company says its SpaceX partnership and other compute deals let it raise usage limits for Claude Code and the Claude API.
   - The usage-limit changes are described as effective immediately.
   - The announcement targets heavy Claude users who were constrained by rate limits or peak-hour reductions.
2. Claude Code receives the most visible user-facing changes.
   - Five-hour Claude Code rate limits are doubled for Pro, Max, Team, and seat-based Enterprise plans.
   - Peak-hour limit reductions are removed for Claude Code on Pro and Max accounts.
   - Anthropic also says Claude Opus API rate limits are being raised considerably.
3. The SpaceX agreement is large and near-term.
   - Anthropic says it will use all compute capacity at SpaceX’s Colossus 1 data center.
   - The deal provides more than 300 MW of new capacity and over 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs within the month.
   - Anthropic says this added capacity will directly improve Claude Pro and Claude Max capacity.
4. The deal sits inside a larger multi-provider compute strategy.
   - Anthropic cites an up-to-5 GW Amazon agreement, including nearly 1 GW of new capacity by the end of 2026.
   - It also cites a 5 GW Google and Broadcom agreement beginning in 2027, plus $30 billion of Azure capacity through Microsoft and NVIDIA.
   - The company says it trains and runs Claude across AWS Trainium, Google TPUs, and NVIDIA GPUs.
5. The infrastructure plan includes geographic and political constraints.
   - Anthropic says enterprise customers in regulated sectors need in-region infrastructure for compliance and data residency.
   - It plans some international expansion, including additional Amazon-supported inference capacity in Asia and Europe.
   - The company says it is focusing on democratic countries, secure supply chains, local investment, and possible extensions of its electricity-price commitment.

## 📝 상세 정리

### 1. Agentic Engineering

1. 글은 먼저 “바이브 코딩”의 의미를 좁혀 정의한다.
   - Osmani는 바이브 코딩을 프롬프트를 넣고, 생성 코드를 받아들이고, 실행한 뒤, 오류를 다시 넣는 방식이라고 정의한다.
   - 그는 이 방식이 프로토타입, 해커톤 데모, 개인 스크립트, 학습, 창의적 탐색에는 유용하다고 본다.
   - 경계는 코드 소유권이다. 사람이 결과를 설명하거나 유지보수할 수 없다면 엔지니어링이 아니다.
2. 핵심 문제는 하나의 용어가 서로 다른 실천을 덮고 있다는 점이다.
   - “바이브 코딩”은 빠른 실험과 규율 있는 전문 워크플로 모두에 쓰이고 있다.
   - Osmani는 이런 혼동이 경영진과 팀이 위험 수준을 잘못 이해하게 만들어 실제 운영상 피해를 낳는다고 주장한다.
   - 같은 AI 산출물도 주말 데모에서는 무해할 수 있지만 결제 시스템이나 운영 서비스에서는 위험할 수 있다.
3. “Agentic engineering”은 전문적 흐름을 가리키는 이름으로 제안된다.
   - 이 용어는 구현, 테스트, 수정을 수행하는 코딩 에이전트를 사람이 지휘하는 방식을 설명한다.
   - 사람이 직접 입력하는 코드가 적더라도 아키텍트, 리뷰어, 의사결정자 역할은 여전히 사람이 맡는다.
   - Osmani는 이 말이 전문적으로 이해 가능하고 “vibe engineering”보다 더 뚜렷한 경계를 만든다고 본다.
4. 이 워크플로는 평범한 엔지니어링 규율에 의존한다.
   - 팀은 에이전트에게 구현을 맡기기 전에 계획, 설계 문서, 명세부터 만든다.
   - 에이전트에게 범위가 정해진 작업을 주고, 결과 코드는 pull request 수준의 엄격함으로 검토한다.
   - 테스트는 신뢰하기 어려운 에이전트 반복을 더 믿을 수 있는 개발 루프로 바꾸는 핵심 장치다.
5. 경험 수준에 따른 효과는 균등하지 않다.
   - 시니어 엔지니어는 약한 아키텍처, 보안 문제, 취약한 추상화를 빠르게 볼 수 있어 더 큰 이득을 얻는다.
   - 주니어는 코드를 이해하기 전에 산출물을 만들 수 있으므로 역량 약화 위험이 생긴다.
   - Osmani의 결론은 AI 보조 개발이 기본기를 덜 중요하게 만드는 것이 아니라 더 중요하게 만든다는 것이다.

### 2. Google unveils Whoop-like screenless Fitbit Air

1. Google은 Fitbit Air를 더 단순한 웨어러블 범주로 포지셔닝한다.

   - Google의 메시지는 스마트워치식 상호작용보다 편안함과 지속 착용에 초점을 둔다.

   - Fitbit Air는 24시간 심박수 모니터링과 심방세동 알림을 포함한 심장 리듬 모니터링을 제공한다.
   - 혈중산소, 안정시 심박수, 심박변이도, 수면 단계, 수면 시간도 추적한다.
   - 일반 활동과 운동은 자동 감지되며, Google은 사용자 습관을 학습할수록 개인화가 개선된다고 말한다.

   - TechCrunch에 따르면 Fitbit Air는 Fitbit Luxe보다 25%, Inspire 3보다 50% 작다.
