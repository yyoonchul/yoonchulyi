### 1. Meta debuts the Muse Spark model in a "ground-up overhaul" of its AI

1. Meta is framing Muse Spark as the visible start of a broader reset in its AI strategy.
   - TechCrunch describes the release as Meta's "first step" in an overhaul of its AI efforts.
   - It is the first model shipped under Meta Superintelligence Labs rather than the older Llama-led storyline.
   - The article ties that reorganization to Mark Zuckerberg's dissatisfaction with how Meta had been trailing ChatGPT and Claude.
2. The organizational reboot is backed by unusually large talent and capital moves.
   - Meta recruited former Scale AI co-founder and CEO Alexandr Wang to lead Meta Superintelligence Labs.
   - It also invested $14.3 billion in Scale AI for a 49% stake, signaling that this is not a cosmetic rename.
   - TechCrunch also notes Meta has recruited researchers from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google to strengthen the team.
3. Muse Spark is already a user-facing product, not just a research announcement.
   - The model is available immediately on the web and inside the Meta AI app.
   - Meta says it plans to add a "Contemplating" mode for more complex problems over time.
   - The company says that mode will let multiple AI agents work in parallel so the system can spend more test-time reasoning without drastically increasing latency.
4. Meta is positioning the product around practical assistant use cases, but the packaging leaves open questions.
   - The company highlights health questions as one category where Muse Spark could help users.
   - It also claims strong performance on visual STEM problems, with examples like creating minigames and troubleshooting home appliances.
   - TechCrunch notes that competitors often reserve their more capable reasoning systems for paid tiers, while Meta has not yet said whether Muse Spark will follow that pattern.

   - Users need an existing Meta account such as Facebook or Instagram to log into Muse Spark.
   - TechCrunch says Meta does not explicitly spell out whether personal account information will feed the AI experience.
   - Because Meta already trains on public user data and is pitching a "personal superintelligence" product, the article says those login requirements could trigger privacy concerns.

### 2. AI Can't Read an Investor Deck

1. Mercor's paper starts from the fear that finance work is near the top of AI automation risk.
   - It cites a Wall Street Journal report that 23% of job-seeking Harvard MBAs were still unemployed three months after graduation.
   - It also points to Anthropic research that placed financial analysts among the ten occupations most exposed to AI displacement.
   - Mercor's claim is that benchmark-level optimism about AI in finance gets overstated when the inputs stop looking clean and structured.
2. The benchmark is designed to mirror real analyst work instead of sanitized QA tasks.
   - Mercor built 25 tasks from real earnings reports, investor presentations, roadmap slides, and regulatory fee schedules.
   - Each task asks the model to identify values from a document and compute a single numeric answer such as a margin, growth rate, dilution percentage, or ratio.
   - Because each problem has one correct number, scoring is pass-or-fail rather than subjective.
3. The evaluation isolates reading failures from reasoning failures.
   - Every task has an image-only version using the original document page and a text-only version that writes the same information out in plain text.
   - That setup separates "can the model read the document?" from "can the model do the math once the values are explicit?"
   - Mercor ran the same prompt across GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Claude Opus 4.6 for 50 evaluations per model, or 25 tasks across both variants.
4. The results show that document reading is the bigger bottleneck than raw financial calculation.
   - In a sanity check with only the question and no source material, Claude Opus 4.6 got 1 of 25 correct, GPT-5.4 got 1 of 25 correct, and Gemini 3.1 Pro got 0 of 25 correct.
   - On text-only tasks, Gemini 3.1 Pro reached 80% accuracy, Claude Opus 4.6 reached 76%, and GPT-5.4 reached 72%.
   - On image-only tasks, performance dropped to a 56%-64% range, which translated to a 16-point drop for Gemini 3.1 Pro and GPT-5.4 and a 20-point drop for Claude Opus 4.6.
5. Mercor says two recurring failure modes explain the collapse on realistic finance inputs.
   - The first is visual extraction: models anchor to the wrong element in dense charts and pull a plausible but incorrect number.
   - The second is reasoning: even with correct inputs, models sometimes use the wrong operation, such as absolute difference instead of percentage change or an inverted ratio.
   - Mercor's example task showed all three models answered the text-only version with $4.00, but on the image-only version Gemini 3.1 Pro stayed correct while Claude returned $2.00 and GPT-5.4 answered $4.30, supporting the claim that messy visual finance documents remain a real weakness.

### 2. AI Can't Read an Investor Deck

1. Mercor의 글은 금융 업무가 AI 자동화 위험 상단에 있다는 불안감에서 출발합니다.
   - 글은 졸업 3개월 후에도 구직 중인 Harvard MBA가 23%였다는 Wall Street Journal 보도를 인용합니다.
   - 동시에 Anthropic 연구가 financial analysts를 AI 대체 노출도가 높은 상위 10개 직업 중 하나로 분류했다고 짚습니다.
   - Mercor의 주장은 입력이 더럽고 복잡해지는 순간, 금융 분야 AI에 대한 낙관이 과장될 수 있다는 것입니다.
2. 이 벤치마크는 정제된 QA 문제가 아니라 실제 애널리스트 업무를 닮도록 설계됐습니다.
   - Mercor는 실제 earnings reports, investor presentations, roadmap slides, regulatory fee schedules에서 25개 과제를 만들었습니다.
   - 각 과제는 문서에서 값을 찾아 margin, growth rate, dilution percentage, ratio 같은 단일 숫자 답을 계산하게 만듭니다.
   - 정답이 하나의 숫자로 고정돼 있기 때문에 평가는 주관식 해석이 아니라 pass-or-fail로 이뤄집니다.
3. 평가 설계는 읽기 실패와 추론 실패를 분리하려는 데 초점을 둡니다.
   - 모든 과제에는 원본 문서 페이지를 그대로 쓰는 image-only 버전과, 같은 정보를 평문으로 옮긴 text-only 버전이 있습니다.
   - 이렇게 하면 "모델이 문서를 읽을 수 있는가?"와 "값이 명시되면 계산은 할 수 있는가?"를 나눠 볼 수 있습니다.
   - Mercor는 GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Opus 4.6에 동일한 프롬프트를 적용해 모델당 50회 평가, 즉 25개 과제의 두 변형을 모두 실행했습니다.
4. 결과는 순수 계산보다 문서 읽기가 더 큰 병목이라는 점을 보여줍니다.
   - 질문만 주고 소스 문서를 주지 않은 sanity check에서 Claude Opus 4.6과 GPT-5.4는 각각 25개 중 1개만 맞혔고, Gemini 3.1 Pro는 0개를 맞혔습니다.
   - text-only 조건에서는 Gemini 3.1 Pro가 80%, Claude Opus 4.6이 76%, GPT-5.4가 72% 정확도를 기록했습니다.
   - image-only 조건에서는 성능이 56%-64%대로 떨어졌고, Gemini 3.1 Pro와 GPT-5.4는 16%포인트, Claude Opus 4.6은 20%포인트 하락했습니다.
5. Mercor는 현실적인 금융 입력에서 무너지는 이유를 두 가지 반복 패턴으로 설명합니다.
   - 첫째는 visual extraction 문제로, 모델이 빽빽한 차트에서 잘못된 요소를 기준점으로 잡고 그럴듯하지만 틀린 숫자를 끌어옵니다.
   - 둘째는 reasoning 문제로, 올바른 값을 받아도 절대 차이를 퍼센트 변화 대신 쓰거나 비율을 뒤집는 식으로 잘못된 연산을 적용합니다.
   - 예시 과제에서는 세 모델이 text-only 버전에서 모두 $4.00을 맞혔지만, image-only 버전에서는 Gemini 3.1 Pro만 정답을 유지했고 Claude는 $2.00, GPT-5.4는 허용 오차를 조금 벗어난 $4.30을 내서 복잡한 시각 금융 문서가 여전히 약점임을 보여줬습니다.
