## 📝 Detailed Notes

### 1. Anthropic’s Claude popularity with paying consumers is skyrocketing

1. TechCrunch frames the story around payment behavior, not vague app-download momentum.
   - The analysis is based on billions of anonymized credit-card transactions covering roughly 28 million U.S. consumers.
   - The dataset came from Indagari, which TechCrunch used to estimate subscription trends rather than total user counts.
   - That focus matters because it tracks users who are actually paying Anthropic, not just trying Claude casually.
2. The clearest signal is a sharp acceleration in early 2026.
   - TechCrunch says consumers began paying for Claude in record numbers between January and February.
   - Anthropic told TechCrunch that Claude paid subscriptions have more than doubled this year.
   - Indagari also said that returning users came back to Claude in record numbers during February.
3. The article gives several reasons why Claude became much more visible in the same window.
   - Anthropic ran Super Bowl ads that mocked ChatGPT's move to show ads and promised Claude would not do the same.
   - Public attention also intensified after late-January reporting on Anthropic's conflict with the U.S. Department of Defense.
   - TechCrunch notes that new-user growth was especially pronounced between those reports and CEO Dario Amodei's February 26, 2026 public statement.
4. Product changes, not just publicity, are presented as real subscription drivers.
   - The article says Claude Code and Claude Cowork, both released in January, helped push paid growth.
   - Anthropic also told TechCrunch that the newly released Computer Use feature triggered another surge.
   - Those features matter commercially because they are not available to free-tier users, giving consumers a reason to upgrade.
5. The growth narrative comes with material limits and caveats.
   - Indagari says most new subscribers are choosing Claude Pro at $20 per month rather than the $100 or $200 tiers.
   - The data has a roughly two-week delay, but TechCrunch says numbers through early March still show growth continuing.
   - The article closes by emphasizing that Claude remains well behind ChatGPT in consumer scale, while the dataset still excludes enterprise revenue and free users.
